Let's get started

Create a bookmark in a few simple steps

This tutorial is designed for users who have just registered and have not yet created any bookmarks. The steps described here are only needed to create the first bookmark. From now on, you will be able to add bookmarks in one step instead of four. Please note that in addition to creating a bookmark manually, you can install the Weblinks.click Google Chrome extension and use it to add bookmarks automatically and directly from the page you want to add a bookmark to.

Bookmarks are organized using three levels of categorization: page, row, column. When you create a bookmark for the first time, you need to create a page, then a row within a page, then a column within a row, and finally a bookmark within a column. The terms "bookmark" and "link" mean the same thing here.

Step 1. Create a page.

Once you're logged in and haven't created any bookmarks yet, you'll see an "Add Page" button on your toolbar.

Click on the "Add Page" button to open a form

Enter "services" in the "Page Path" field and "My services" in the "Caption" field, then click the "Submit" button. The "My services" page will be created and shown in the toolbar.

Step 2. Create a row.

Click on the "Add row" button to open a form

Enter "Online services" in the "Caption" field and then click the "Submit" button. The "Online services" row will be created and shown in the "My Services" page.

Step 3. Create a column.

Click on the "Add column" button to open a form

Enter "Google services" in the "Caption" field and then click the "Submit" button. The "Google services" column will be created and shown in the "Online services" row.

Step 4. Create a bookmark.

Click on the "Add link" button to open a form

Enter "https://www.google.com/" in the "Url" field and "Google search" in the "Label" field and then click the "Submit" button. The "Google search" bookmark will be created and shown in the "Google services" column.

Now click on the "Google search" link. The Google page will open in a separate tab.

Congratulations! You've just created your first bookmark!
